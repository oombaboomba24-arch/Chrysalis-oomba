# Chime

## Business Overview
Chime is a US-based neobank and consumer fintech company that recently completed a US IPO. It offers fee-free banking products — checking, savings, credit builder, earned wage access (MyPay), and instant loans — primarily targeting underbanked and working-class Americans. Revenue is driven by interchange fees and newer subscription/premium tiers (Chime Prime). Q1 2026: 10.2M active users, $53M net income (first GAAP profitable quarter), 25% YoY revenue growth, full-year 2026 guidance of $2.66–2.69B. ([Business of Apps, 2026](https://www.businessofapps.com/data/chime-statistics/); [PYMNTS, 2026](https://www.pymnts.com/earnings/2026/chime-leans-on-ai-pushes-upmarket-in-first-profitable-quarter/))

### PM Perspective

- **Product Strategy Signals:** Chime's identity challenge is consumer-scale authentication for a post-IPO neobank under scrutiny — they had a data breach in April 2026 and are aggressively expanding product scope. This creates genuine urgency around identity, authentication hardening, fraud prevention, and account lifecycle.
- **Roadmap & Direction:** Moving upmarket with Chime Prime, adding new products (loans, EWA), and navigating post-IPO compliance pressure. Identity/auth is foundational to all of these — sign-in, account recovery, fraud signals, and cross-product continuity.

- **Fit Assessment:** Strong match. Consumer fintech identity with a breach context is a compelling problem to walk into. Anand's experience at TTD (consumer identity from scratch, telemetry, experimentation) is directly transferable. Note: Chime is US-only which works for San Diego residency.
